Every week, I hear the same tired moan: “LinkedIn is dead,” or “Indeed is just a graveyard for my resume.” People love to blame the platforms for their lack of offers.

Here’s the thing: it’s not the platform’s fault you aren't getting hired.

The market is just a brutal, crowded mess, and everyone is diving for the same job postings at the exact same time. If you’re just sitting on the big boards, you’re playing the lottery. You aren’t building a real job search strategy; you’re just waiting for a miracle.

## Why are you still betting on one horse?

To be honest, the idea that one job board is "better" than another is a total myth. They’re all just digital notice boards. When you limit yourself to just one or two, you’re cutting yourself off from the majority of the market.

I’ve noticed that the people actually getting interviews are the ones who have mastered a targeted job search. They are hitting niche boards, digging into company career pages, and checking more sources rather than waiting for the "Easy Apply" feed. But there’s a catch.

## Who actually has time for this?

Staying on top of 5 to 10 different websites, refreshing pages every few hours, and hoping for a fresh listing? It’s a full-time job in itself. It’s draining, it’s boring, and it’s a recipe for burnout. I might be wrong, but I don't think any human being was designed to stare at job boards all day.

You need to stop acting like a search engine and start acting like a strategist. If you want to know how to apply for jobs effectively, you have to stop the manual grind.
